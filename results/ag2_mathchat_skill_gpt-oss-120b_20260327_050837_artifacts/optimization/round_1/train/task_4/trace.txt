### 2.  The cyclic condition for \(A,D,H,G\)

Four points \((x_i,y_i)\) are concyclic iff the determinant  

\[
\begin{vmatrix}
x_i & y_i & x_i^{2}+y_i^{2} & 1
\end{vmatrix}=0
\]

vanishes.  
Using the coordinates of \(A(0,0), D(0,16), H(x,33), G(x+184,33)\) we obtain

\[
\begin{vmatrix}
0 & 0 & 0 & 1\\
0 & 16 & 256 & 1\\
x & 33 & x^{2}+1089 & 1\\
x+184 & 33 & (x+184)^{2}+1089 & 1
\end{vmatrix}=0 .
\]

Expanding the determinant (or, equivalently, using the 3‑by‑3 sub‑determinant after
removing the first row and column) gives

\[
x^{2}+184x-561 = 0 .
\]
