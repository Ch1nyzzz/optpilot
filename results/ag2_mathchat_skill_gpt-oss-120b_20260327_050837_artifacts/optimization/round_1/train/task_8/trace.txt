### 1.  Geometry of the rhombus  

Let the four vertices of the rhombus be  

\[
A=(-\mathbf u),\qquad C=\mathbf u,\qquad 
B=-\mathbf v,\qquad D=\mathbf v ,
\]

where  

\[
\mathbf u=(x_1,y_1),\qquad \mathbf v=(x_2,y_2)
\]

are points on the hyperbola  

\[
\frac{x^{2}}{20}-\frac{y^{2}}{24}=1 .
\]

Because the diagonals intersect at the origin, opposite vertices are opposite
vectors.  
The side vectors are  

\[
\overrightarrow{AB}= \mathbf v-\mathbf u,\qquad 
\overrightarrow{BC}= -\mathbf u-\mathbf v .
\]

For a rhombus the two side lengths are equal, i.e.

\[
|\mathbf v-\mathbf u|^{2}=|-\mathbf u-\mathbf v|^{2}.
\]

Expanding gives  

\[
\underbrace{x_1^{2}+y_1^{2}}_{|\mathbf u|^{2}}+
\underbrace{x_2^{2}+y_2^{2}}_{|\mathbf v|^{2}}-
2(x_1x_2+y_1y_2)=
\underbrace{x_1^{2}+y_1^{2}}_{|\mathbf u|^{2}}+
\underbrace{x_2^{2}+y_2^{2}}_{|\mathbf v|^{2}}+
2(x_1x_2+y_1y_2),
\]

hence  

\[
x_1x_2+y_1y_2=0\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad \mathbf u\perp\mathbf v .
\]

Thus the two diagonals are **perpendicular**.

The length of diagonal \(BD\) is  

\[
BD = 2|\mathbf v|,\qquad\text{so}\qquad BD^{2}=4|\mathbf v|^{2}=4\bigl(x_2^{2}+y_2^{2}\bigr).
\]

Hence we have to **minimise** \(|\mathbf v|^{2}\) under the conditions  

\[
\begin{cases}
\displaystyle \frac{x_1^{2}}{20}-\frac{y_1^{2}}{24}=1,\\[4pt]
\displaystyle \frac{x_2^{2}}{20}-\frac{y_2^{2}}{24}=1,\\[4pt]
x_1x_2+y_1y_2=0 .
\end{cases}
\]

### 2.  Parametrising the hyperbola  

Write the hyperbola in its standard hyperbolic‑trigonometric form  

\[
x=\sqrt{20}\,\cosh t,\qquad y=\sqrt{24}\,\sinh t\qquad (t\in\mathbb R).
\]

Thus  

\[
\mathbf u =\bigl(a\cosh t_1,\; b\sinh t_1\bigr),\qquad
\mathbf v =\bigl(a\cosh t_2,\; b\sinh t_2\bigr),
\]

with  

\[
a=\sqrt{20},\qquad b=\sqrt{24}.
\]

The orthogonality condition becomes  

\[
a^{2}\cosh t_1\cosh t_2+b^{2}\sinh t_1\sinh t_2=0 .
