We are asked for the probability that the *last* word in this final list contains the letter \(G\).

### 1.  What determines the last word?

The alphabetical order of the words is decided first by the **first** letter of each word, and only if those are equal by the second letter.

The largest possible first letter among the twelve letters is **\(L\)**.  
Whenever \(L\) appears in a word, that word must begin with \(L\) (the pair is written in alphabetical order).  
Since \(L\) occurs exactly once, there is **exactly one** word that begins with \(L\).

Consequently, the word that starts with \(L\) is automatically the lexicographically greatest word, i.e. the *last* word in the list.

Thus the last word is simply the pair that contains the letter \(L\).

### 2.  When does the last word contain \(G\)?

In any other pairing of \(L\) (with a letter different from \(G\)) the last word does **not** contain \(G\).

> What is the probability that, in a random pairing of the twelve letters, \(G\) is paired with \(L\)?
