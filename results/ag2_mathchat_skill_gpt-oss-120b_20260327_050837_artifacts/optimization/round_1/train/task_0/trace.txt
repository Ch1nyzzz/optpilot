Output: **Solution (step‑by‑step)**  

1. **Set up the equations**  

   * Distance of the walk: \(d = 9\) km.  
   * Let the walking speed be \(s\) km / h and the coffee‑shop time be \(t\) minutes.  

   When she walks at speed \(s\):
   \[
   \frac{9}{s}\text{ (hours)} + \frac{t}{60}\text{ (hours)} = 4\text{ h}.
   \tag{1}
   \]

   When she walks at speed \(s+2\):
   \[
   \frac{9}{s+2} + \frac{t}{60}= 2\text{ h }24\text{ min}=2.4\text{ h}.
   \tag{2}
   \]

2. **Eliminate \(t\)**  

   Subtract (2) from (1):
   \[
   \frac{9}{s}-\frac{9}{s+2}=4-2.4=1.6.
   \]

   The left‑hand side simplifies:
   \[
   \frac{9\big((s+2)-s\big)}{s(s+2)}=\frac{18}{s(s+2)}.
   \]

   Hence
   \[
   \frac{18}{s(s+2)}=1.6\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
   s(s+2)=\frac{18}{1.6}=11.25.
   \]

3. **Solve for \(s\)**  

   \[
   s^{2}+2s-11.25=0.
   \]

   Using the quadratic formula:
   \[
   s=\frac{-2\pm\sqrt{4+45}}{2}
     =\frac{-2\pm7}{2}.
   \]

   The positive root gives the feasible speed:
   \[
   s=\frac{-2+7}{2}= \frac{5}{2}=2.5\text{ km/h}.
   \]

4. **Find the coffee‑shop time \(t\)**  

   From (1):
   \[
   \frac{9}{2.5}+ \frac{t}{60}=4
   \;\Longrightarrow\;
   3.6 + \frac{t}{60}=4
   \;\Longrightarrow\;
   \frac{t}{60}=0.4
   \;\Longrightarrow\;
   t=0.4\times60=24\text{ minutes}.
   \]

   (Check with the second scenario: \(9/(2.5+2)=9/4.5=2\) h, plus 24 min = 2.4 h, as required.)

5. **Compute the total time at speed \(s+\frac12\)**

   \[
   \frac{9}{3}=3\text{ h}=180\text{ min}.

   180\text{ min}+24\text{ min}=204\text{ min}.
