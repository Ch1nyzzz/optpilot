The probability of her winning the grand prize given that she won a prize is $\tfrac{m}{n}$ where $m$ and $n$ are relatively prime positive integers. Find $m+n$.
Jen enters a lottery by picking $4$ 
Output: **Solution Overview**

Let  

* \(H\) be the fixed set of 4 numbers Jen chooses.  
* \(R\) be the random set of 4 numbers drawn from \(\{1,\dots ,10\}\).  

All \(\binom{10}{4}=210\) possible \(R\) are equally likely.

Define the events  

* \(A\): “Jen wins a prize”  \(\iff |H\cap R|\ge 2\).  
* \(B\): “Jen wins the grand prize” \(\iff |H\cap R|=4\).

We need the conditional probability  

\[
P(B\mid A)=\frac{P(B)}{P(A)} .
\]

Thus we must count how many random sets \(R\) give each possible size of the intersection \(|H\cap R|\).

---

### Counting by intersection size  

For a fixed \(k\) (the number of matches), we must:

1. Choose which \(k\) of Jen’s 4 numbers appear in \(R\): \(\binom{4}{k}\) ways.  
2. Choose the remaining \(4-k\) numbers of \(R\) from the 6 numbers **not** in \(H\): \(\binom{6}{4-k}\) ways.

Hence  

\[
\#\{R:|H\cap R|=k\}= \binom{4}{k}\binom{6}{4-k},\qquad k=0,1,2,3,4 .
\]

Compute each:

| \(k\) | Count |
|------|-------|
| 0 | \(\binom{4}{0}\binom{6}{4}=1\cdot15=15\) |
| 1 | \(\binom{4}{1}\binom{6}{3}=4\cdot20=80\) |
| 2 | \(\binom{4}{2}\binom{6}{2}=6\cdot15=90\) |
| 3 | \(\binom{4}{3}\binom{6}{1}=4\cdot6=24\) |
| 4 | \(\binom{4}{4}\binom{6}{0}=1\cdot1=1\) |

Check: \(15+80+90+24+1 = 210\), the total number of possible \(R\).

---

### Probabilities of the relevant events  

* Grand prize (\(B\)): exactly 4 matches → count = 1.  

  \[
  P(B)=\frac{1}{210}.
  \]

* Winning a prize (\(A\)): at least 2 matches → counts for \(k=2,3,4\).

  \[
  \#A = 90+24+1 = 115,\qquad
  P(A)=\frac{115}{210}.
  \]

---

### Conditional probability  

\[
P(B\mid A)=\frac{P(B)}{P(A)}=
\frac{\frac{1}{210}}{\frac{115}{210}}=
\frac{1}{115}.
\]

The fraction \(\frac{1}{115}\) is already in lowest terms, so \(m=1,\; n=115\).

\[
m+n = 1+115 = \boxed{116}.
\]

# Total ways to draw 4 numbers from 10
total = comb(10, 4)

# Count draws with exactly k matches between Jen's set and random draw
counts = {}
for k in range(5):
    # Choose k numbers from Jen's 4 that will appear,
    # and choose the remaining 4-k numbers from the 6 numbers she didn't pick.
    counts[k] = comb(4, k) * comb(6, 4 - k)

# Number of draws that give at least two matches (prize)
prize_count = sum(counts[k] for k in range(2, 5))

# Number of draws that give all four matches (grand prize)
grand_count = counts[4]

# Conditional probability = grand prize / prize
prob = grand_count / prize_count

# Reduce fraction
from fractions import Fraction
frac = Fraction(prob).limit_denominator()
m, n = frac.numerator, frac.denominator
result = m + n

print(result)   # 116
```
