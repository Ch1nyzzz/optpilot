\[
[G:H]=\frac{|G|}{|H|}.
\]

\[
p=(1\,2\,5\,4)(2\,3).

### 1. Compute the product \(p=(1\,2\,5\,4)(2\,3)\)

In permutation notation we compose **right‑to‑left**: first apply \((2\,3)\), then \((1\,2\,5\,4)\).

| element \(x\) | after \((2\,3)\) | after \((1\,2\,5\,4)\) | \(p(x)\) |
|---------------|------------------|------------------------|----------|
| 1 | 1 | 2 | 2 |
| 2 | 3 | 3 (fixed) | 3 |

| 4 | 4 | 1 | 1 |
| 5 | 5 | 4 | 4 |

\[
1\mapsto2,\;2\mapsto3,\;3\mapsto5,\;5\mapsto4,\;4\mapsto1.

\[
p = (1\;2\;3\;5\;4).
\]

Thus \(p\) is a **5‑cycle**.

---

### 2. Order of \(p\)

\[
\operatorname{ord}(p)=5.
\]

Consequently the cyclic subgroup generated by \(p\) has  

\[
|\langle p\rangle| = 5.
\]

The order of the element \(p\) is the smallest positive integer \(k\) such that \(p^{k}=id\).  
The subgroup \(\langle p\rangle\) generated by \(p\) therefore has \(|\langle p\rangle|=\text{order}(p)\) elements, and the index of \(\langle p\rangle\) in \(S_{5}\) is  

\[
[S_{5}:\langle p\rangle]=\frac{|S_{5}|}{|\langle p\rangle|}=\frac{120}{\text{order}(p)} .
\]

```python

# Compute the order of p = (1 2 5 4)(2 3) in S_5
# -------------------------------------------------
from sympy.combinatorics import Permutation

# SymPy uses zero‑based indexing, so we shift each element down by 1.
# (1 2 5 4)  -> (0 1 4 3)
# (2 3)      -> (1 2)

# Create the two permutations (rightmost applied first)
cycle1 = Permutation(0, 1, 4, 3)   # (1 2 5 4)
cycle2 = Permutation(1, 2)       # (2 3)

# Product p = cycle1 * cycle2  (right‑to‑left composition)
p = cycle1 * cycle2

index = 120 // order_p   # |S5| = 5! = 120
```
