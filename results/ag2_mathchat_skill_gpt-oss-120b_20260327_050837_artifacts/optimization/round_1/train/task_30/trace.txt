We want the degree  

\[
\bigl[\,\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3},\sqrt{18}) : \mathbb{Q}\,\bigr].
\]

---

### 1. Simplify the generating set  

\[
\sqrt{18}= \sqrt{9\cdot 2}=3\sqrt{2}.
\]

Thus \(\sqrt{18}\in\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})\).  
Consequently  

\[
\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3},\sqrt{18})
   =\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3}).
\]

So we only need the degree of the bi‑quadratic extension \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3})\).

---

### 2. Check whether \(\sqrt{3}\) already lies in \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})\)

\(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})\) consists of numbers \(a+b\sqrt{2}\) with \(a,b\in\mathbb{Q}\).  
If \(\sqrt{3}=a+b\sqrt{2}\) for some rationals \(a,b\), squaring gives  

\[
3 = a^{2}+2b^{2}+2ab\sqrt{2}.
\]

Since \(\sqrt{2}\) is irrational, the coefficient of \(\sqrt{2}\) must be zero: \(2ab=0\).  
Either \(a=0\) or \(b=0\).

* If \(a=0\): then \(3 = 2b^{2}\) → \(b^{2}=3/2\) → \(b\notin\mathbb{Q}\).
* If \(b=0\): then \(3 = a^{2}\) → \(a=\pm\sqrt{3}\notin\mathbb{Q}\).

Both possibilities are impossible, so \(\sqrt{3}\notin\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})\).

Hence \([\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3}) : \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})]=2\).

---

### 3. Compute the total degree  

\[
\begin{aligned}
\bigl[\,\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3}) : \mathbb{Q}\,\bigr]
&= \bigl[\,\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3}) : \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})\,\bigr]\;
   \bigl[\,\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2}) : \mathbb{Q}\,\bigr] \\
&= 2 \times 2 = 4 .
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### 4. Answer  

The degree of the extension \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2},\sqrt{3},\sqrt{18})\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\) is **4**.

\[
\boxed{\text{(B) } 4}
\]
