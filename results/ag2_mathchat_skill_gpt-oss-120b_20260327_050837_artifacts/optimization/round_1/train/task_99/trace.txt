### 1.  What “shiny’’ means  

For a fixed \(n\)-tuple \(\mathbf x=(x_{1},\dots ,x_{n})\) denote  

\[
F(\sigma)=\sum_{i=1}^{n-1}x_{\sigma(i)}x_{\sigma(i+1)},
\qquad\sigma\in S_{n}.
\]

The tuple is *shiny* iff  

\[
F(\sigma)\ge -1\qquad\text{for every permutation }\sigma .
\tag{1}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  A family attaining \(-1\)

Take  

\[
\mathbf x=(1,-1,0,0,\dots ,0)\in\mathbb R^{n}\qquad (n\ge 3).
\]

In any permutation the two non‑zero numbers are either consecutive
or they are separated by at least one zero.  
Hence the sum of the adjacent products is either \(-1\) (when they are
consecutive) or \(0\) (when they are not).  Thus (1) holds.
The total sum of all pairwise products equals  

\[
\sum_{1\le i<j\le n}x_i x_j = 1\cdot(-1) = -1 .
\tag{2}
\]

Therefore the constant we are looking for cannot be larger than \(-1\).

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  A universal lower bound

Let  

\[
S:=\sum_{1\le i<j\le n}x_i x_j .
\]

Count the total of the quantities \(F(\sigma)\) over all \(n!\) permutations.
Each unordered pair \(\{i,j\}\) appears as an adjacent pair in exactly  

\[
2\,(n-1)!
\]

permutations (the two possible orders of the pair, the remaining
\(n-2\) elements can be placed arbitrarily).  Consequently  

\[
\sum_{\sigma\in S_n}F(\sigma)=2\,(n-1)!\,S .
\tag{3}
\]

Because of (1) every term of the left–hand side is at least \(-1\); hence  

\[
2\,(n-1)!\,S\ge -\,n! .
\]

\[
S\ge -\frac{n}{2}.

import itertools, random, math, sys
def min_sum_for_n(n, trials=200000):
    best = 1e9
    best_vec = None
    for _ in range(trials):
        # random vector in [-2,2]
        x = [random.uniform(-2,2) for _ in range(n)]
        # check shiny condition
        shiny = True
        for perm in itertools.permutations(range(n)):
            s = sum(x[perm[i]]*x[perm[i+1]] for i in range(n-1))
            if s < -1 - 1e-9:   # allow tiny tolerance
                shiny = False
                break
        if shiny:
            total = sum(x[i]*x[j] for i in range(n) for j in range(i+1,n))
            if total < best:
                best = total
                best_vec = x[:]
    return best, best_vec

for n in range(3,6):
    best, vec = min_sum_for_n(n, trials=50000)
    print(n, best, vec)
