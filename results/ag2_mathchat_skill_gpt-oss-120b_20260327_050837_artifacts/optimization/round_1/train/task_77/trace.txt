### 1.  Reduction to a square  

The statement is invariant under affine transformations:  

* an affine map sends a convex quadrilateral to any other convex quadrilateral,  
* it multiplies every area by the same factor, therefore the ratio  

\[
\frac{S}{S_{1}}
\]

does not change.

Consequently we may assume that the outer quadrilateral is the unit square  

\[
A(0,0),\;B(1,0),\;C(1,1),\;D(0,1).
\]

Write  

\[
A_{1}=(t,0),\qquad B_{1}=(1,u),\qquad C_{1}=(v,1),\qquad D_{1}=(0,w),
\qquad 0\le t,u,v,w\le 1 .
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  The four corner triangles  

\[
\begin{aligned}
T_{1}&=\operatorname{area}(AA_{1}D_{1}) =\frac12\,t\,w,\\[2mm]
T_{2}&=\operatorname{area}(BB_{1}A_{1}) =\frac12\,(1-t)\,u,\\[2mm]
T_{3}&=\operatorname{area}(CC_{1}B_{1}) =\frac12\,(1-v)\,(1-u),\\[2mm]
T_{4}&=\operatorname{area}(DD_{1}C_{1}) =\frac12\,v\,(1-w).
\end{aligned}
\]

The inner quadrilateral is the complement of the four corner triangles in the
unit square, hence  

\[
S_{1}=1-(T_{1}+T_{2}+T_{3}+T_{4}) .
\]

Denote the four numbers by  

\[
a\le b\le c\le d\qquad (a,b,c,d\in\{T_{1},T_{2},T_{3},T_{4}\}),
\]

so that  

\[
S=a+b,\qquad\qquad T:=T_{1}+T_{2}+T_{3}+T_{4}=a+b+c+d .
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  What has to be proved  

The required inequality  

\[
k\,S_{1}\ge S\qquad\text{for all }t,u,v,w
\]

is equivalent to  

\[
\frac{S}{S_{1}}\le k .
\]

Thus the smallest admissible \(k\) is the **maximum** possible value of the
ratio \(\displaystyle\frac{S}{S_{1}}\).  
Because \(S_{1}=1-T\),

\[
\frac{S}{S_{1}}=\frac{a+b}{1-(a+b+c+d)} .
\]

Consequently we have to show  

\[
\boxed{a+b\le\frac12\bigl(1-(a+b+c+d)\bigr)}\qquad\Longleftrightarrow\qquad
2(a+b)+(a+b+c+d)\le1 .
\tag{1}
\]
