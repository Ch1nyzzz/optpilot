bisector of \(\angle ABC\).

For a circle of radius \(r\)

* the distance from \(B\) to its centre is \(\displaystyle \frac{r}{\sin(\theta/2)}\);
* two successive circles of the same radius are \(2r\) apart along the
  bisector.

Hence, if we can place \(N\) equal circles of radius \(r\) in a row,
the distance from \(B\) to the centre of the last circle is  

\[
d(N,r)=\frac{r}{\sin(\theta/2)}+2r\,(N-1).
\tag{1}
\]

The same distance must be the same for any choice of radius, because it
is determined only by the geometry of the triangle (the two sides and the
third side \(AC\)).  
We are told that

* with \(r=34\) we can place \(N=8\) circles,
* with \(r=1\) we can place \(N=2024\) circles.

\[
\frac{34}{\sin(\theta/2)}+2\cdot34\,(8-1)

\frac{1}{\sin(\theta/2)}+2\cdot1\,(2024-1).
\]

\[
