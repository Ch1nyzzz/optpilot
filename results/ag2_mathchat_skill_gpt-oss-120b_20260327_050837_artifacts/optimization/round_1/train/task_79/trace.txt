### Proof  

Write  

\[
H(n)=f(n)-g(n),\qquad n\ge 1 .
\]

The given relation becomes  

\[
\tag{E_n}\qquad
f^{\,g(n)+1}(n)+g^{\,f(n)}(n)=H(n+1)+1 .
\]

Because the codomain is the set of positive integers, every iterate is a
positive integer; consequently  

\[
f^{\,g(n)+1}(n)\ge f(n),\qquad g^{\,f(n)}(n)\ge g(n) .
\tag{1}
\]

Hence  

\[
f(n)+g(n)\le H(n+1)+1 = f(n+1)-g(n+1)+1 .
\tag{2}
\]

#### 1.  The function \(g\) is constantly \(1\)

\[
m=\min\{n\ge 1\mid g(n)\ge 2\}.
\]

\[
f^{\,2}(m-1)+1 = f(m)-g(m)+1\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
f^{\,2}(m-1)=f(m)-g(m)\le f(m)-2 .
\tag{3}
\]

Since \(f^{\,2}(m-1)=f\bigl(f(m-1)\bigr)\ge f(m-1)\), (3) yields  

\[
f(m-1)\le f(m)-2\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad f(m)\ge f(m-1)+2 .
\tag{4}
\]

Now apply \((E_m)\).  Using (1),

\[
f^{\,g(m)+1}(m)+g^{\,f(m)}(m)\ge f^{\,2}(m)\ge f(m) .

\[
f(m)\le f(m+1)-g(m+1)+1\le f(m+1) .
\tag{5}
\]

\[
f(m+k)\ge f(m-1)+2(k+1) . \tag{6}
