\[

D&=(4,0), &E&=(20,0),\\[2mm]
F&=\frac{13}{91}C=\Bigl(\frac{13x}{91},\frac{13y}{91}\Bigr),\\[2mm]

\end{aligned}
\]

The reflections are obtained by the midpoint property:

\[
\begin{aligned}
M&=2F-D=\Bigl(\frac{26x}{91}-4,\; \frac{26y}{91}\Bigr),\\[2mm]
N&=2E-G=\Bigl(40-\frac{65x}{91},\; -\frac{65y}{91}\Bigr).
\end{aligned}
\]

### 2.  Using the area of \(DEGF\)

The quadrilateral \(DEGF\) has vertices \(D(4,0),E(20,0),G, F\) in that order.  
Because \(D\) and \(E\) lie on the \(x\)-axis, the shoelace formula simplifies dramatically:

\[
\begin{aligned}
[\!DEGF\!]&=\frac12\Bigl|
20\cdot\frac{65y}{91}-4\cdot\frac{65y}{91}
\Bigr|
      =\frac12\cdot\frac{1248y}{91}
      =\frac{624y}{91}.
\end{aligned}
\]

We are told \([\!DEGF\!]=288\), hence  

\[
\frac{624y}{91}=288\quad\Longrightarrow\quad y=42 .
\]

Thus the height of the triangle is \(y=42\).

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  Determining the \(x\)-coordinate of \(C\)

\[
x^{2}+y^{2}=91^{2}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
x^{2}+42^{2}=91^{2}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
x^{2}=6517 .
\]

(The exact value of \(x\) will not be needed later.)
