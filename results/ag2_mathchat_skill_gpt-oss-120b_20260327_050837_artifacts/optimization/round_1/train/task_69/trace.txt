### 1.  Geometry of a regular \(2n\)-gon  

Write the points as complex numbers  

\[
z_{k}=x_{k}+i y_{k}\qquad(k=1,\dots ,2n).
\]

a centre \(c\in\mathbb C\), a radius \(r\ge 0\) and a permutation
\(\sigma\in S_{2n}\) such that  

\[
z_{k}=c+r\,\omega^{\sigma(k)},\qquad 
\omega=e^{\frac{\pi i}{n}}\;( \omega^{2n}=1).
\tag{1}
\]

Conversely, if (1) holds for some \(c,r,\sigma\) then the points are either
all equal (\(r=0\)) or they are the vertices of a regular \(2n\)-gon.

Put  

\[
w_{k}=z_{k}-c .
\]

Equation (1) says that the multiset \(\{w_{1},\dots ,w_{2n}\}\) is
\(\{r\omega^{0},r\omega^{1},\dots ,r\omega^{2n-1}\}\) (up to a permutation).
Hence the monic polynomial having the \(w_{k}\)’s as roots is  

\[
\prod_{k=1}^{2n}(t-w_{k})=t^{2n}-r^{2n}.
\tag{2}
\]

The coefficients of the left–hand side are the elementary symmetric
functions \(e_{m}(w_{1},\dots ,w_{2n})\;(m=1,\dots ,2n-1)\).
Comparing (2) with the right–hand side we obtain the **characterising
equations**

\[
e_{m}(w_{1},\dots ,w_{2n})=0\qquad(m=1,\dots ,2n-1).
\tag{3}
\]

\[

import sympy as sp
