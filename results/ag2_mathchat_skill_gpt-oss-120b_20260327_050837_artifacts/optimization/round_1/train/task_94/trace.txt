\(n\) colours  

\[
C_{1},C_{2},\dots ,C_{n},
\]

it is possible to place \(m\) marbles on a circle so that **every**
\(n+1\) consecutive marbles contain at least one marble of each colour
\(C_i\;(i=1,\dots ,n)\).

--------------------------------------------------------------------
### 1.  A necessary condition

Let a circular arrangement satisfying the condition be given and let  

\[
a_i\;(i=1,\dots ,n)
\]

be the number of marbles of colour \(C_i\) that occur on the circle.
Denote by  

\[
d_{i,1},d_{i,2},\dots ,d_{i,a_i}
\]

the (cyclic) distances between two consecutive marbles of colour
\(C_i\); each \(d_{i,j}\) is a positive integer and  

\[
d_{i,1}+d_{i,2}+\dots +d_{i,a_i}=m . \tag{1}
\]

If for some colour \(C_i\) a distance \(d_{i,j}\) were larger than
\(n+1\), then the block of \(n+1\) marbles that starts just after the
first occurrence and ends just before the second one would contain no
marble of colour \(C_i\) – a contradiction.  
Hence  

\[
d_{i,j}\le n+1\qquad\text{for all }i,j . \tag{2}
\]

From (1) and (2) we obtain  

\[
m\le a_i\,(n+1)\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad 
a_i\ge \Bigl\lceil\frac{m}{\,n+1\,}\Bigr\rceil . \tag{3}
\]

Summation over all colours gives  

\[
m=a_1+\dots +a_n\ge
n\Bigl\lceil\frac{m}{\,n+1\,}\Bigr\rceil . \tag{4}
\]

Write \(m=(n+1)q+r\) with \(0\le r\le n\) (\(q=\big\lfloor\frac{m}{n+1}\big\rfloor\)).
Then \(\big\lceil\frac{m}{n+1}\big\rceil = q\) if \(r=0\) and
\(=q+1\) if \(r>0\).

\[
\begin{cases}
nq\le (n+1)q+r &\text{if }r=0,\\[2mm]
n(q+1)\le (n+1)q+r &\text{if }r>0 .
\end{cases}
\]

n\le q+r . \tag{5}
