   - Each radical has **two equivalent nitrogen nuclei**.  
   - The nuclear spin of ^14N is \(I = 1\).  

2. **Hyperfine pattern for one radical**  
   - For \(N\) equivalent nuclei of spin \(I\) the number of EPR lines is  
     \[
     2NI + 1 .
     \]
   - Here \(N = 2\) and \(I = 1\) → \(2NI+1 = 2\times2\times1+1 = 5\).  
   - A monoradical therefore shows a **quintet** (5 lines).

3. **Consider the diradical**  
   - The diradical contains **two such radicals**, i.e. a total of **four equivalent nitrogens**.  
   - The exchange coupling constant \(J\) is **much smaller than the hyperfine constant \(a\) ( \(J \ll a\) )**.  
   - In this limit the exchange does **not average** the hyperfine interactions; the two electrons behave essentially independently, and the hyperfine splittings simply add.

4. **Number of lines for the whole system**  
   - With four equivalent nuclei (\(N = 4\), \(I = 1\)) the same rule applies:  
     \[
     \text{lines} = 2NI + 1 = 2 \times 4 \times 1 + 1 = 9 .
     \]
   - Equivalently, the spectrum is the convolution of two quintets (5 × 5), which also yields 9 distinct positions.

5. **Conclusion**  
   - The EPR spectrum of a rigid nitronyl nitroxide **diradical** with \(J \ll a\) will display **nine lines**.

**Answer: (B) 9 lines**
