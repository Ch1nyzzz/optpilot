### 1.  Reformulating the condition  

Put  

\[
x=\frac{a}{b},\qquad y=\frac{b}{c},\qquad z=\frac{c}{d},\qquad w=\frac{d}{a}.
\]

Then  

\[
xyzw=1\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad w=\frac1{xyz}.
\]

Dividing the given equality  

\[
(a+c)(b+d)=ac+bd
\]

by \(bd\) we obtain  

\[
\Bigl(\frac{a}{b}+\frac{c}{b}\Bigr)\Bigl(\frac{b}{d}+1\Bigr)=\frac{ac}{bd}+1 .
\]

Because  

\[
\frac{c}{b}= \frac{c}{d}\frac{d}{b}=z\frac{1}{y z}= \frac1y ,\qquad 
\frac{b}{d}= \frac{b}{c}\frac{c}{d}=y z ,
\]

the condition becomes  

\[
\boxed{(x+\frac1y)(yz+1)=xz+1}\tag{1}
\]

with \(x,y,z>0\).

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  The optimisation problem  

We have to minimise  

\[
S=x+y+z+w=x+y+z+\frac1{xyz}\tag{2}
\]

subject to (1).  

Because the problem is homogeneous, we may treat \(x,y,z\) as free
variables and keep (1) as the only constraint.

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  Lagrange multipliers  

Define  

\[
\mathcal L(x,y,z,\lambda)=x+y+z+\frac1{xyz}
      +\lambda\bigl[(x+\frac1y)(yz+1)-xz-1\bigr].
\]

\[
\begin{cases}
\displaystyle\frac{\partial\mathcal L}{\partial x}=1-\frac1{x^{2}yz}
