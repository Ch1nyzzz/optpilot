1. **Interpret the convexity condition**  
   For a strictly convex function \(f\), the graph between any two points lies **strictly below** the chord joining those points.  
   Hence for any \(a<b\) and any \(x\) with \(a<x<b\),

   \[
   f(x) \;<\; \frac{b-x}{b-a}\,f(a)+\frac{x-a}{b-a}\,f(b).
   \]

2. **Apply it to the given points**  
   The function passes through \((-2,5)\) and \((2,9)\).  
   The chord through these points is the line

   \[
   y = \frac{9-5}{2-(-2)}(x+2)+5 = 1\cdot x + 7,
   \]
   i.e. \(y = x+7\).

3. **Upper bound for \(f(1)\)**  
   Since \(1\) lies between \(-2\) and \(2\),

   \[
   f(1) \;<\; (1)+7 = 8 .
   \]

   Thus \(f(1)\) must be **strictly less than 8**.

4. **No lower bound**  
   We can construct strictly convex functions that satisfy the two given points and make \(f(1)\) arbitrarily low.

   \[
   f_k(x)=k x^{2}+x+(7-4k)

   \[
   f_k(1)=8-3k,
   \]

5. **Range of possible values**

   \[
