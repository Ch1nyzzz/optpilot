\[
\mathbf a=(a_{1},a_{2},\dots ,a_{60}),\qquad a_i\in \mathbb Z_{\ge 1}.
\]

\[
L_k=\{B_1,\dots ,B_k\},\qquad R_k=\{B_{k+1},\dots ,B_{60}\}.
\]

Alice then decides which side receives “\(+1\)” and which side receives “\(-1\)”.
Hence the change of the vector \(\mathbf a\) is either  

\[
\mathbf s_k=(\underbrace{+1,\dots ,+1}_{k},

\]

### 2.  Looking at the *differences*  

Define the (signed) differences between consecutive boxes  

\[
d_i = a_i-a_{i+1}\qquad (i=1,\dots ,59).
\]

From the description of a move we obtain a very simple rule:

*All the differences stay unchanged **except** the one that corresponds to the cut*.
If the cut is at \(k\) and Alice decides to give “\(+1\)” to the left side,
then  

\[
d_k\longrightarrow d_k+2 .
\]

If she gives “\(+1\)” to the right side, then  

\[
d_k\longrightarrow d_k-2 .
\]

Thus in every round **exactly one** of the 59 numbers \(d_i\) is altered,
and it is altered by \(\pm 2\).
