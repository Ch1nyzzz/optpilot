For a rational number to have a *finite* decimal expansion (be “short”) its denominator, after cancelling common factors with the numerator, must be of the form  

\[
2^{a}\,5^{b}\qquad(a,b\ge 0).
\]

Hence for a positive integer \(m\) a positive integer \(t\) is **\(m\)-tastic** iff there is a
\(c\in\{1,\dots ,2017\}\) such that  

* all prime factors of \(c\,m\) different from \(2\) and \(5\) divide \(10^{t}-1\);
* for every \(k<t\) at least one of those primes does **not** divide \(10^{k}-1\).

The same \(c\) must work for all the inequalities, i.e. the condition is
“the smallest exponent for which every odd prime \(\neq5\) dividing \(c\,m\) divides
\(10^{t}-1\)”.
