### 1.  Reformulating the second condition  

\[
\begin{aligned}
\sum_{\text{sym}} a^{2}b
&=a^{2}b+a^{2}c+b^{2}a+b^{2}c+c^{2}a+c^{2}b  \\
&=(a+b+c)(ab+bc+ca)-3abc .
\end{aligned}
\]

Indeed  

\[
\begin{aligned}
(a+b+c)(ab+bc+ca)
&=a(ab+bc+ca)+b(ab+bc+ca)+c(ab+bc+ca)\\
&=a^{2}b+a^{2}c+ab^{2}+b^{2}c+ac^{2}+bc^{2}+3abc .
\end{aligned}
\]

\[
S_{1}=a+b+c,\qquad S_{2}=ab+bc+ca,\qquad S_{3}=abc,

\[
\boxed{S_{1}=300},

\[
100\,S_{2}-S_{3}=2\,000\,000\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad 
\boxed{S_{3}=100S_{2}-2\,000\,000 } .
\]

### 2.  Bounding \(S_{2}\)

For non‑negative numbers with fixed sum \(S_{1}=300\),

* the **minimum** of \(S_{2}=ab+bc+ca\) is \(0\) (e.g. \((300,0,0)\));
* the **maximum** occurs at the most “balanced’’ triple, i.e. \(a=b=c=100\):

\[
S_{2}^{\max}=3\cdot100^{2}=30\,000 .
\]

Because \(S_{3}\ge 0\),

\[
100S_{2}-2\,000\,000\ge 0\;\Longrightarrow\; S_{2}\ge 20\,000 .
\]

Thus  

\[
\boxed{20\,000\le S_{2}\le 30\,000 } .
\]

Moreover \(S_{3}=100(S_{2}-20\,000)\) is a multiple of \(100\).

### 3.  Reducing the problem to a finite search  

The three numbers \(a,b,c\) are the (non‑negative) integer roots of  

\[
x^{3}-S_{1}x^{2}+S_{2}x-S_{3}=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
x^{3}-300x^{2}+S_{2}x- \bigl(100S_{2}-2\,000\,000\bigr)=0 .
\]

For each integer \(S_{2}\) in the interval \([20\,000,30\,000]\) we can compute  

\[
P_{S_{2}}(x)=x^{3}-300x^{2}+S_{2}x-100S_{2}+2\,000\,000 .
\]

If this cubic factors over the integers into three non‑negative roots (allowing repetitions), those roots give a valid triple \((a,b,c)\).

Because the interval for \(S_{2}\) contains only \(10\,001\) values, a direct computer search is trivial.
