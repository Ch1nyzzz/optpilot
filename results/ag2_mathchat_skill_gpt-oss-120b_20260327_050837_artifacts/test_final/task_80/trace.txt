### 2.  First consequences  

Denote  

\[
P(x,y):\;f(x+f(xy))+y=f(x)f(y)+1 .
\]

\[
P(x,1):\qquad f(x+f(x))=f(x)f(1) .\tag{2}
\]

\[
c:=f(1)>0 .
\]

\[
f(x+f(x))=c\,f(x)\qquad\forall x>0 .\tag{3}

\[
P(1,y):\qquad f(1+f(y))=c\,f(y)+1-y .\tag{4}

### 3.  A useful symmetric identity  

From \(P(x,y)\) and \(P(y,x)\) we obtain  

\[
\begin{aligned}
f(x+f(xy)) &= f(x)f(y)+1-y,\\
f(y+f(xy)) &= f(y)f(x)+1-x .
\end{aligned}
\]

Subtracting the two equalities gives  

\[
\boxed{\,f(x+f(xy))-f(y+f(xy))=x-y\;}\qquad\forall x,y>0 .\tag{5}
\]

\[
g(t):=f(t)-t\qquad(t>0).
\]

\[
g\bigl(x+f(xy)\bigr)=g\bigl(y+f(xy)\bigr)\qquad\forall x,y>0 .\tag{6}
\]

### 2.  Trying a linear form  

Because the equation mixes the arguments \(x,\;y\) and the product \(xy\), a natural first guess is a linear function  

\[
f(x)=ax+b\qquad (a,b>0).
\]

* \(x+f(xy)=x+ axy+b = x(1+ay)+b\).

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{LHS}&=f\bigl(x+f(xy)\bigr)+y\\
          &=a\bigl[x(1+ay)+b\bigr]+b+y\\

\end{aligned}
\]

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{RHS}&=f(x)f(y)+1\\
          &=(ax+b)(ay+b)+1\\
          &=a^{2}xy+abx+aby+b^{2}+1 .
\end{aligned}
\]

monomials to be equal:

| term | LHS coefficient | RHS coefficient |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| \(xy\) | \(a^{2}\) | \(a^{2}\) |
| \(x\)  | \(a\)    | \(ab\) |
| \(y\)  | \(1\)    | \(ab\) |
| constant | \(ab+b\) | \(b^{2}+1\) |

With \(b=1\) the third row gives \(1 = a\).  
Finally the constant term check: \(a b + b = a+1 = 2\) and \(b^{2}+1 = 2\); they agree.

Thus  

\[
a=1,\qquad b=1\quad\Longrightarrow\quad f(x)=x+1 .
