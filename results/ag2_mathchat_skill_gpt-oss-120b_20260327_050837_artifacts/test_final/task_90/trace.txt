### 1.  Notation  

For a statement \(P(x,y)\) we denote  

\[
P(x,y):\;f\bigl(x+f(x+y)\bigr)+f(xy)=x+f(x+y)+y\,f(x)\qquad(\forall x,y\in\mathbb R).
\]

Put  

\[
c:=f(0).
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  Two elementary consequences  

* **\(y=0\).**  

  \[
  P(x,0)\;:\;f\bigl(x+f(x)\bigr)+c=x+f(x) .
  \tag{1}
  \]

  Hence  

  \[
  f\bigl(x+f(x)\bigr)=x+f(x)-c\qquad(\forall x).      \tag{1'}
  \]

* **\(x=0\).**  

  \[
  P(0,y)\;:\;f\bigl(f(y)\bigr)+c=f(y)+y\,c .
  \tag{2}
  \]

  Consequently  

  \[
  f\bigl(f(y)\bigr)=f(y)+(y-1)c\qquad(\forall y).      \tag{2'}
  \]

From (1') with \(x=0\) we obtain  

\[
f(c)=0. \tag{3}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  Determination of the constant \(c\)

Apply (2') with \(y=c\) and use (3):

\[
f\bigl(f(c)\bigr)=f(0)+(c-1)c\Longrightarrow 0=c+(c-1)c .
\]

Thus  

\[
c\bigl(c-2\bigr)=0\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad c=0\ \text{or}\ c=2 .
\tag{4}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 4.  Linearisation  

From (1') we have  

\[
f\bigl(x+f(x)\bigr)-\bigl(x+f(x)\bigr)=-c .
\tag{5}
\]

Define  

\[
g(x):=x+f(x)\qquad(\forall x).
\]

Equation (5) becomes  

\[
f\bigl(g(x)\bigr)=g(x)-c . \tag{6}
\]

Now apply (2') to the argument \(g(x)\) and use (6):

\[
\begin{aligned}
f\bigl(f(g(x))\bigr)
   &=f\bigl(g(x)-c\bigr)                       \\
   &=\bigl(g(x)-c\bigr)+(g(x)-c-1)c          \qquad\text{by (2')}\\
   &=g(x)-c+g(x)c-c^{2}-c .
\end{aligned}
\tag{7}
\]

On the other hand, using (2') with \(y=g(x)\),

\[
f\bigl(f(g(x))\bigr)=f(g(x))+(g(x)-1)c=g(x)-c+(g(x)-1)c .
\tag{8}
\]

Equating (7) and (8) and simplifying gives  

\[
g(x)c=c^{2}\qquad(\forall x). \tag{9}
\]

Because \(c\) is a constant, (9) forces \(g\) to be constant **or** \(c=0\)
