We are given a triangle \(ABC\) with  

* circumradius \(R=13\) (center \(O\)),  
* inradius \(r=6\) (center \(I\)),  
* the segment \(IA\) (the internal bisector of \(\angle A\)) is perpendicular to \(OI\).

We are asked for the product \(AB\cdot AC\).

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 1.  Useful formulas  

* Euler’s relation for the distance between circum‑ and in‑center  

\[
OI^{2}=R(R-2r).
\]

* Length of the bisector from a vertex to the incenter  

\[
AI=\frac{r}{\sin \frac{A}{2}} .
\]

* Law of sines  

\[
a=BC=2R\sin A,\qquad 
b=CA=2R\sin B,\qquad 
c=AB=2R\sin C .
\]

* Inradius in terms of the circumradius  

\[
r=4R\sin\frac{A}{2}\sin\frac{B}{2}\sin\frac{C}{2}.
\]

* Area \(\Delta\) and semiperimeter \(s\)  

\[
\Delta = rs = \frac{abc}{4R}= \frac12 bc\sin A .
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  Determining \(\displaystyle \sin\frac{A}{2}\)

Because \(IA\perp OI\), the triangle \(OAI\) is right‑angled at \(I\); hence  

\[
OA^{2}=OI^{2}+AI^{2}.
\]

We have  

\[
OA=R=13,\qquad OI^{2}=R(R-2r)=13(13-12)=13.
\]

Thus  

\[
AI^{2}=R^{2}-OI^{2}=13^{2}-13=156\quad\Longrightarrow\quad AI=2\sqrt{39}.
\]

But also \(AI=\dfrac{r}{\sin\frac{A}{2}}\); therefore  

\[
\frac{6}{\sin\frac{A}{2}}=2\sqrt{39}
\Longrightarrow
\sin\frac{A}{2}= \frac{3}{\sqrt{39}}=\frac{\sqrt{39}}{13}.
\]

Consequently  

\[
\cos\frac{A}{2}= \sqrt{1-\sin^{2}\frac{A}{2}}
               =\sqrt{1-\frac{39}{169}}
               =\frac{\sqrt{130}}{13}.
\]

Hence  

\[
\sin A =2\sin\frac{A}{2}\cos\frac{A}{2}
       =2\cdot\frac{\sqrt{39}}{13}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{130}}{13}
       =\frac{2\sqrt{30}}{13},
\qquad
\cos A =1-2\sin^{2}\frac{A}{2}= \frac{7}{13}.
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  The side \(a=BC\)

From the law of sines  

\[
a=2R\sin A = 2\cdot13\cdot\frac{2\sqrt{30}}{13}=4\sqrt{30}.
