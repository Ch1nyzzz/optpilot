### 2.  The case \(n=2\) gives an upper bound  

For \(n=2\) write \(t:=\dfrac{x_2}{x_1}>1\;(x_2=t\,x_1)\).  
Then  

\[
\frac{1}{x_1}+\frac{1}{x_2-x_1}

\frac{2}{x_1}+\frac{3}{x_2}
   =\frac{1}{x_1}\Bigl(2+\frac{3}{t}\Bigr).
\]

Hence  

\[
R(t):=
\frac{\displaystyle\frac{1}{x_1}+\frac{1}{x_2-x_1}}
     {\displaystyle\frac{2}{x_1}+\frac{3}{x_2}}
 =\frac{t^{2}}{(t-1)(2t+3)}
 =\frac{t^{2}}{2t^{2}+t-3},\qquad t>1 .
\]

\(R(t)\) attains its minimum when  

\[
\frac{d}{dt}R(t)=0\Longleftrightarrow t(t-6)=0,
\]

i.e. at \(t=6\).  The minimal value is  

\[
