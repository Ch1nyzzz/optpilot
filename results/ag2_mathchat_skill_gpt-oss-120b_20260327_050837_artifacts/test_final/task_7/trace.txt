### 1.  Geometry of a shifted torus  

Put the sphere’s centre at the origin and let the axis of the torus be the line  

\[
\ell :\;(x,y,z) = (h,0,0) + t\,(0,0,1) ,\qquad h\ge 0 .
\]

Thus the axis is parallel to the \(z\)-axis and its distance from the sphere’s centre is the (unknown) number \(h\).

For a point \((x,y,z)\) let  

\[
\rho' = \sqrt{(x-h)^2+y^{2}}
\]

be its distance to the axis \(\ell\).  
The torus is the set of points whose distance to the axis equals \(R\) up to the tube‑radius \(a\); equivalently  

\[
\bigl(\rho'-R\bigr)^{2}+z^{2}=a^{2}. \tag{1}
\]

The sphere is  

\[
x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2}=R_{s}^{2}=121 . \tag{2}
\]
