\[

\]

\[
s_1<s_2<\dots <s_k ,\qquad

s_i<t_i .
\]

A pair of checkpoints \((p,q)\;(p<q)\) is *linked* by the company iff there is a
sequence  

\[
p=s_{i_0}=t_{i_1}=s_{i_1}=t_{i_2}= \dots =s_{i_{r-1}}=t_{i_r}=q
\]

with \(i_0<i_1<\dots <i_r\); i.e. we can ride a car, get off at its
finish, board the next car whose start coincides with that finish, and so on.
Consequently the set of linked pairs of a company is exactly  

\[
\mathcal L=\{(s_i,t_j)\mid i\le j\text{ and }t_{i}=s_{i+1}= \dots =s_{j}\}.
\]

In particular every linked pair uses a **start** and a **finish** that belong
to the same company.  
Hence a company can involve at most \(2k\) different checkpoints
(the \(k\) starts and the \(k\) finishes).
