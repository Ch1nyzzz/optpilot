The maximum is attained, for example, by the alternating choice  

\[
x_{1}=1,\;x_{2}=-1,\;x_{3}=1,\;x_{4}=-1,\dots ,x_{2n}=(-1)^{2n-1}.

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 1.  Re‑writing the sum  

For a fixed distance  

\[
d=s-r\qquad (1\le d\le 2n-1)
\]

there are exactly \(2n-d\) ordered pairs \((r,s)\) with \(s-r=d\).
Hence

\[
\begin{aligned}
F(\mathbf x)
&:=\sum_{1\le r<s\le 2n}(s-r-n)x_{r}x_{s}   \\
&=\sum_{d=1}^{2n-1}(d-n)\sum_{r=1}^{2n-d}x_{r}x_{r+d}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{1}
\]

Denote  

\[
C_{d}(\mathbf x)=\sum_{r=1}^{2n-d}x_{r}x_{r+d}\qquad (1\le d\le 2n-1).
\]

Because \(|x_{i}|\le 1\),

\[
|C_{d}(\mathbf x)|\le \sum_{r=1}^{2n-d}|x_{r}||x_{r+d}|
               \le 2n-d .
\tag{2}
\]

The bound (2) is sharp: it is attained exactly when all the products
\(x_{r}x_{r+d}\) have the same sign (either all \(+1\) or all \(-1\)).

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  An upper bound for the whole sum  

Split the distances into the two groups  

\[
\begin{aligned}
&\mathcal D_{-}=\{d\mid 1\le d\le n-1\}, &&\text{(here }d-n<0\text{)}\\[2pt]
&\mathcal D_{+}=\{d\mid n+1\le d\le 2n-1\}, &&\text{(here }d-n>0\text{)} .
\end{aligned}
\]

For \(d\in\mathcal D_{-}\) we would like \(C_{d}(\mathbf x)\) to be **negative**
so that the product \((d-n)C_{d}\) becomes positive;  
for \(d\in\mathcal D_{+}\) we would like \(C_{d}(\mathbf x)\) to be **positive**.

Using (2) we obtain

\[
\begin{aligned}
F(\mathbf x)
&\le \sum_{d\in\mathcal D_{-}} (n-d)\,(2n-d)
   \;+\;\sum_{d\in\mathcal D_{+}} (d-n)\,(2n-d)\\[4pt]
&= \sum_{t=1}^{\,n-1} t\,(n-t)\;+\;\sum_{t=1}^{\,n-1} t\,(n-t)\\[4pt]
&=2\sum_{t=1}^{n-1}t\,(n-t) .
\end{aligned}
\tag{3}
\]

The elementary sum is

\[
\sum_{t=1}^{n-1}t\,(n-t)
   =n\sum_{t=1}^{n-1}t-\sum_{t=1}^{n-1}t^{2}
   =n\frac{(n-1)n}{2}-\frac{(n-1)n(2n-1)}{6}

F(\mathbf x)=\sum_{1\le r<s\le 2n}(s-r-n)\,x_r x_s ,\qquad

### 1.  Reformulation of the sum  

Write  

\[
s-r-n=(s-n)-r .
\]

Define  

\[
a_r=r,\qquad b_s=s-n .
\]

Then  

\[
F(\mathbf y)=\sum_{r<s}(b_s-a_r)\,y_r y_s
          =\sum_{r<s}b_s y_r y_s-\sum_{r<s}a_r y_r y_s .
\]

For a fixed index \(k\) let  

\[
S_k=\sum_{i=1}^{k}y_i ,\qquad
Y=S_{2n}=\sum_{i=1}^{2n}y_i .
\]

The two double sums can be written with the help of the partial
sums:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\sum_{r<s}b_s y_r y_s
   &=\sum_{s=2}^{2n} b_s y_s\Bigl(\sum_{r=1}^{s-1}y_r\Bigr)
    =\sum_{s=1}^{2n} b_s y_s S_{s-1},\\[2mm]
\sum_{r<s}a_r y_r y_s
   &=\sum_{r=1}^{2n-1} a_r y_r\Bigl(\sum_{s=r+1}^{2n}y_s\Bigr)
    =\sum_{r=1}^{2n} a_r y_r\bigl(Y-S_{r}\bigr).
\end{aligned}
\]

Consequently  

\[
\boxed{%
F(\mathbf y)=\sum_{k=1}^{2n} y_k\Bigl[\,b_k S_{k-1}-a_k\bigl(Y-S_{k}\bigr)\Bigr]
}
\tag{1}
\]
