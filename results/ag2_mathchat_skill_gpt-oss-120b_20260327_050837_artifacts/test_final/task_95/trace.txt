Let  

\[
P(m,n):\qquad f\bigl(f(m)+n\bigr)+f(m)=f(n)+f(3m)+2014\qquad(m,n\in\mathbb Z)
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------
### 1.  A first translation property  

Put \(m=0\) in \(P\):

\[
f\bigl(f(0)+n\bigr)+f(0)=f(n)+f(0)+2014\Longrightarrow 
f\bigl(n+f(0)\bigr)=f(n)+2014\tag{1}
\]

Denote  

\[
c:=f(0).
\]

Then (1) says  

\[
\boxed{f(x+c)=f(x)+2014\qquad\forall x\in\mathbb Z}\tag{2}
\]

Iterating,  

\[
f(x+kc)=f(x)+2014k\qquad(k\in\mathbb Z). \tag{3}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------
### 2.  Linear part of \(f\)

From (2) we obtain that the “difference” of \(f\) for a shift by \(c\) is a
constant.  
Hence the slope of a possible linear part must be  

\[
a:=\frac{2014}{c}\in\mathbb Z,
\]

because \(c\mid 2014\) (otherwise the right–hand side of (2) would not be an
integer for all \(x\)).  

Write  

\[
f(x)=ax+g(x),\qquad x\in\mathbb Z, \tag{4}
\]

where \(g:\mathbb Z\to\mathbb Z\) is a new function.  
Using (2) and (4),

\[
ax+ag(x)+c a+g(x+c)=ax+g(x)+2014
\]

gives  

\[
g(x+c)=g(x)\qquad\forall x, \tag{5}
\]

i.e. **\(g\) is periodic with period \(c\).**
