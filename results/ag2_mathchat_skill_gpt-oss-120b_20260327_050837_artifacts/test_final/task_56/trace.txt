Output: **Solution Overview**

We need to evaluate two statements about groups and their homomorphic images.

---

### Statement 1  
*Every homomorphic image of a group \(G\) is isomorphic to a factor (quotient) group of \(G\).*

**Reasoning**

- Let \(\varphi : G \to H\) be any group homomorphism.
- Its image \(\operatorname{Im}\varphi\) is a subgroup of \(H\) and is called a *homomorphic image* of \(G\).
- The **First Isomorphism Theorem** (or Fundamental Homomorphism Theorem) tells us that  

  \[
  G/\ker\varphi \;\cong\; \operatorname{Im}\varphi .
  \]

- The group \(G/\ker\varphi\) is precisely a *factor group* (quotient group) of \(G\) (the kernel \(\ker\varphi\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\)).
- Hence every homomorphic image \(\operatorname{Im}\varphi\) is isomorphic to the factor group \(G/\ker\varphi\).

**Conclusion:** Statement 1 is **true**.

---

### Statement 2  
*The homomorphic images of a group \(G\) are the same (up to isomorphism) as the factor groups of \(G\).*

**Reasoning**

- From Statement 1 (proved above) we already have: every homomorphic image is isomorphic to some factor group.
- Conversely, any factor group \(G/N\) (where \(N\trianglelefteq G\)) is the image of the natural projection homomorphism  

  \[
  \pi_N : G \longrightarrow G/N,\qquad \pi_N(g)=gN .
  \]

  Thus each factor group is itself a homomorphic image of \(G\).
- Therefore there is a one‑to‑one correspondence (up to isomorphism) between the set of homomorphic images of \(G\) and the set of factor groups of \(G\).

**Conclusion:** Statement 2 is **true**.

---

### Final Answer  

Both statements are true, so the correct option is  

**(A) True, True**.
