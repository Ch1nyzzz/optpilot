--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 1.  Intuition

Place the points of \(\mathcal F\) on the real line in increasing order  

\[
x_1 < x_2 < \dots < x_n .
\]

For a fixed point \(x_i\) the distances to the points on its left are
\(x_i-x_j\;(j<i)\) and to the right are \(x_j-x_i\;(j>i)\).  
When we move away from \(x_i\) the distances strictly increase, therefore the
sequence of scales that appear for \(x_i\) is **non‑decreasing** and each new
scale can appear only when we cross a distance that lies in a new dyadic
interval \([2^{d},2^{d+1})\).

Consequently, for a given point the number of different scales equals the
number of dyadic intervals that contain at least one other point of the set.
If a point has at most \(k\) scales, then at most \(k\) different dyadic
intervals are “occupied’’ around it.
