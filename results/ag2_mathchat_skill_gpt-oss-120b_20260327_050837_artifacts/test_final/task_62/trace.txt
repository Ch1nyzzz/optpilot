2. **Interpret the given probabilities**  
   - “30 % probability of rain **and no sun**” means  
     \[
     P(R \cap S^{c}) = 0.30 .
     \]  
   - “40 % probability of **no rain**” means  
     \[
     P(R^{c}) = 0.40 .
     \]

3. **Find the marginal probability of rain**  
   \[
   P(R) = 1 - P(R^{c}) = 1 - 0.40 = 0.60 .
   \]

4. **Use the partition of the rain event**  
   The event “rain” can be split into two mutually exclusive cases:  
   \[
   R = (R \cap S) \;\cup\; (R \cap S^{c}) .
   \]  
   Hence  
   \[
   P(R) = P(R \cap S) + P(R \cap S^{c}) .
   \]

5. **Solve for the probability of rain **and** sun**  
   \[
   P(R \cap S) = P(R) - P(R \cap S^{c})
                = 0.60 - 0.30
                = 0.30 .
   \]

6. **Select the answer**  
   The probability of both rain and sun is **0.30**, which corresponds to option **(C)**.
