### 2.  When does a perfect matching exist?

A perfect matching can be taken from a cycle only when the cycle length is
even, because we must select every vertex exactly once.

\[
\frac{24}{d}\text{ even}\;\Longleftrightarrow\; d\mid 12 .

of length \(3\); no perfect matching is possible.

We have 24 vertices placed on a circle.  
A *segment of equal length* means that every chosen segment connects two vertices that are the same number of steps apart along the circle.  
If the step size is \(k\) ( \(1\le k\le 12\) ), the segment joins vertex \(i\) with vertex \(i+k\) (indices modulo 24).  
Thus all 12 segments must be chords of the same step size \(k\).

For a fixed \(k\) we must pair every vertex with exactly one of its two neighbours at distance \(k\).  
The graph formed by the edges of step‑size \(k\) is a **2‑regular circulant graph**; it decomposes into disjoint cycles.

* Let \(d=\gcd(24,k)\).  
  Then each cycle has length  

\[
L=\frac{24}{d},
\]

and there are exactly \(d\) such cycles (because \(24\) vertices are split evenly).

* A perfect matching (pairing of all vertices) exists on a cycle **iff** the cycle length is even.  
  Since \(L\) is even for every divisor \(d\) of 24 **except** \(d=8\) (which gives \(L=3\)), the only forbidden step size is \(k=8\).

* On an even cycle there are exactly two alternating matchings.  
  Consequently, for a given admissible \(k\) the number of ways to choose the 12 equal‑length segments is  

\[
2^{\text{(# cycles)}} = 2^{d}=2^{\gcd(24,k)} .
\]

* The step sizes \(k\) and \(24-k\) produce the same unordered chords, so we count each length only once, i.e. \(k=1,\dots ,12\).

Putting everything together, the total number of required drawings is  

\[
\sum_{\substack{k=1}^{12}\\k\neq 8} 2^{\gcd(24,k)} .
\]
