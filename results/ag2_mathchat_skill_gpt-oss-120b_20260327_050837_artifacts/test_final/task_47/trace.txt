Output: **Solution**

When a comet of mass \(m\) falls onto the Earth from a very large distance (effectively from infinity) and is released with zero initial speed, the *minimum* speed it can have when it reaches the Earth’s surface is the **escape velocity** of the Earth.  

The escape velocity from a body of mass \(M\) and radius \(R\) is  

\[
v_{\text{esc}}=\sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} .
\]

If the comet arrives with this speed, its kinetic energy just before impact is  

\[
K = \frac12 m v_{\text{esc}}^{2}
    = \frac12 m\left(\frac{2GM}{R}\right)
    = \frac{mGM}{R}.
\]

The gravitational potential energy lost by the comet while falling from infinity to the Earth’s surface is exactly the same amount, so the total energy released in the impact (essentially the kinetic energy at impact) is  

\[
E = \frac12 m\left(\frac{2GM}{R}\right).
\]

**Answer choice:** **(C) \(\displaystyle \frac12\,m\,(2GM/R)\).**
