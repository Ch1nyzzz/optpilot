### 1.  Background facts

* For any integer \(n\), the number of generators (i.e. elements of order \(n\)) in a *cyclic* group of order \(n\) is  
  \[
  \varphi(n),
  \]
  where \(\varphi\) is Euler’s totient function.

* If a group \(G\) contains an element \(a\) of order \(n\), the subgroup \(\langle a\rangle\) generated by \(a\) is cyclic of order \(n\).  
  Consequently, \(\langle a\rangle\) contains exactly \(\varphi(n)\) elements of order \(n\).

* Two distinct cyclic subgroups of the same order intersect only in the identity element.  
  (If they shared a non‑identity element, that element would generate the whole subgroup, forcing the two subgroups to be equal.)

---

### 2.  Apply to \(n = 15\)

* \(15 = 3\cdot5\) and \(\varphi(15)=\varphi(3)\varphi(5)=2\cdot4=8\).

* Hence **any** cyclic subgroup of order 15 has exactly **8** elements of order 15 (the generators).

---
