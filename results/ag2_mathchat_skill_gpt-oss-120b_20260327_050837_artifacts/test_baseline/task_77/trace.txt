### 1.  What the smallest constant must satisfy  

For a fixed \(n\) put  

\[
F_{a}(x)=a(x-1)^{2}+x-\Bigl(\frac{x^{2N}+1}{2}\Bigr)^{\!1/N},
\qquad N=2^{n}.
\]

The required inequality is \(F_{a}(x)\ge 0\) for every real \(x\).
Because the term \(a(x-1)^{2}\) is linear in \(a\), the smallest admissible
\(a\) is  

\[
a_{n}= \sup_{x\neq 1}\;
\frac{\displaystyle\Bigl(\frac{x^{2N}+1}{2}\Bigr)^{\!1/N}-x}
{(x-1)^{2}} .
\tag{1}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  Behaviour near the point \(x=1\)

Write \(x=1+h\;(h\to 0)\).  
Using the binomial expansion we obtain  

\[
\Bigl(\frac{(1+h)^{2N}+1}{2}\Bigr)^{\!1/N}
=1+h+\frac{N}{2}\,h^{2}+O(h^{3}) .
\]

Consequently  

\[
\frac{\displaystyle\Bigl(\frac{x^{2N}+1}{2}\Bigr)^{\!1/N}-x}
{(x-1)^{2}}
=
\frac{1+h+\frac{N}{2}h^{2}+O(h^{3})-(1+h)}{h^{2}}
=
\frac{N}{2}+O(h) .
\]

Hence  

\[
\lim_{x\to 1}\frac{\displaystyle\Bigl(\frac{x^{2N}+1}{2}\Bigr)^{\!1/N}-x}
{(x-1)^{2}}=\frac{N}{2},
\]

so any admissible constant must satisfy  

\[
a_{n}\ge\frac{N}{2}=2^{\,n-1}. \tag{2}
\]
