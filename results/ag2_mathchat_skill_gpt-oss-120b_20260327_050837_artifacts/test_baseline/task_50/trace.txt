Thus, statement **(B)** is the only one that contradicts the established properties of the Box‑Pierce and Ljung‑Box tests.

*Reasoning*  

- The Box‑Pierce statistic \(Q = n\sum_{k=1}^{h}\hat\rho_k^{2}\) and the Ljung‑Box statistic  

  \[
  Q^{*}=n(n+2)\sum_{k=1}^{h}\frac{\hat\rho_k^{2}}{n-k}
  \]

  are both asymptotically \(\chi^{2}_{h}\); therefore statement (A) is correct.  

- The Ljung‑Box modification was introduced precisely because the original Box‑Pierce statistic performs poorly in small samples (it tends to be oversized). Hence the Box‑Pierce test **does not** have better small‑sample properties; statement (B) is false.  

- Because the Box‑Pierce statistic is often oversized in small samples, statement (C) is true.  

- As the sample size grows, both statistics retain the correct asymptotic \(\chi^{2}\) distribution under the null, so they do **not** “always reject” the null when it is true. Statement (D) is therefore also false, but the question asks for the *single* incorrect statement that is most directly contradicted by standard textbook knowledge, which is (B).
