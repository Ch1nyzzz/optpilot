Output: **Solution – Step‑by‑Step Reasoning**

1. **Recall the definition of enthalpy**  
   \[
   H \;=\; U + PV
   \]

2. **Take the total differential** (or finite change) of \(H\):  
   \[
   dH = dU + P\,dV + V\,dP
   \]
   – This is an exact expression that is always true for any infinitesimal change of a closed system.

3. **Identify the given relation**  
   The problem states the relation  
   \[
   \Delta H = \Delta U + P\,\Delta V
   \]
   which is the same as the differential above **without** the \(V\,dP\) (or \(V\Delta P\)) term.

4. **Determine when the missing term vanishes**  
   The term \(V\,dP\) disappears only when the pressure does **not** change during the process, i.e. when the process occurs at **constant pressure** (\(dP = 0\) or \(\Delta P = 0\)).

5. **Conclusion**  
   Therefore the relation \(\Delta H = \Delta U + P\Delta V\) is valid **only for constant‑pressure processes**.

---
