### 1.  Zeros of \(f\)

\[
\sin\!\bigl(7\pi\sin(5x)\bigr)=0
\Longleftrightarrow 7\pi\sin(5x)=m\pi\quad (m\in\mathbb Z)
\Longleftrightarrow \sin(5x)=\frac{m}{7}.
\]

Because \(|\sin(5x)|\le 1\), the integer \(m\) must satisfy \(-7\le m\le 7\).  
Hence the admissible values are  

\[
m\in\{-7,-6,\dots ,0,\dots ,6,7\}\qquad (15\text{ values}).
\]

#### 1.1  Period considerations  

The function \(\sin(5x)\) has period \(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{5}\).  
When \(x\) runs from \(0\) to \(2\pi\), the argument \(5x\) runs from \(0\) to \(10\pi\); that is **five full periods** of \(\sin\).

*If \(|m/7|<1\) and \(m\neq0\):* each period contributes **two** solutions (the usual “up‑and‑down” crossing).  
Thus we obtain \(5\times 2 = 10\) solutions for each such \(m\).

*If \(m=0\):* we solve \(\sin(5x)=0\Rightarrow 5x=k\pi\).  
With \(0<5x<10\pi\) we have \(k=1,2,\dots ,9\) → **9** solutions (the endpoints \(k=0,10\) are excluded because the interval is open).

*If \(|m|=7\):* \(\sin(5x)=\pm1\).  
\(\sin(5x)=1\) occurs at \(5x=\frac{\pi}{2}+2\pi k\);  
\(\sin(5x)=-1\) occurs at \(5x=\frac{3\pi}{2}+2\pi k\).  

Counting the admissible integers \(k\) (so that \(0<5x<10\pi\)) gives **5** solutions for each of \(m=7\) and \(m=-7\).
