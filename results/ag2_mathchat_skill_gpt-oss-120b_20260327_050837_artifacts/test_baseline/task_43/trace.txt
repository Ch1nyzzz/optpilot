   - **(B) “The UK Constitution gives the judiciary the power to overturn acts of parliament.”**  
     - Incorrect. Under parliamentary sovereignty, courts cannot strike down primary legislation; they can only interpret it and, in limited cases (e.g., incompatibility with the European Convention on Human Rights), issue a declaration of incompatibility.

     - Correct. This precisely captures the uncodified nature and the multiplicity of sources (statutes, common law, conventions, etc.).

   - **(D) “The UK Constitution is based on a Bill of Rights.”**
