For a positive integer \(n\) let  

* \(\tau(n)\) – number of positive divisors of \(n\);  
* \(\sigma(n)\) – sum of all positive divisors of \(n\).

We want to know for which \(n\) the multiset of its divisors can be placed in a rectangular
\(r\times c\) table (\(r,c\ge 1,\;rc=\tau(n)\)) so that  

* every divisor occurs exactly once,
* the sum of the numbers in each row is the same,
* the sum of the numbers in each column is the same.

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 1.   Necessary arithmetic conditions  

If such a table exists, let the common row‑sum be \(R\) and the common column‑sum be \(C\).
Adding all rows gives  

\[
rR = \sigma(n) \qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad R = \frac{\sigma(n)}{r}.
\]

Adding all columns gives  

\[
cC = \sigma(n) \qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad C = \frac{\sigma(n)}{c}.
\]

Hence  

\[
r\mid\sigma(n),\qquad c\mid\sigma(n),\qquad rc=\tau(n). \tag{1}
\]

So a **necessary** condition is the existence of a factorisation
\(\tau(n)=rc\) with both \(r\) and \(c\) dividing \(\sigma(n)\).

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.   When is the condition also sufficient?

Condition (1) is also **sufficient** because we can always build a
