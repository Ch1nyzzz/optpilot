Output: **Answer: (C) In theory, the sample could be larger than the population**  

**Reasoning**

- **(A)** True – The population is indeed the entire set of items (people, objects, measurements, etc.) that we are interested in studying.  
- **(B)** True – A population can be infinite (e.g., all possible outcomes of a continuous random variable).  
- **(C)** **False** – By definition a sample is a subset of the population. Even when sampling with replacement, the *distinct* elements drawn cannot exceed the number of distinct elements in the population; the sample size (number of observations) may be larger, but the sample cannot contain more *different* items than exist in the population. Hence the statement that “the sample could be larger than the population” is false.  
- **(D)** True – In a simple random sample each individual in the population has an equal probability of being selected.  

Therefore, the false statement is **(C)**.
