### 1.  Reformulating the condition  

Put  

\[
x=\frac{a}{b},\qquad y=\frac{c}{d},\qquad t=\frac{b}{d}>0 .
\]

Then  

\[
a=xb,\qquad c=yd,\qquad b=td .
\]

The given relation  

\[
(a+c)(b+d)=ac+bd
\]

becomes  

\[
(xb+yd)(b+d)=x y b d+bd .
\]

Dividing by \(bd\;(>0)\) and using \(b=td\) we obtain  

\[
x t +\frac{y}{t}+x+y = xy+1 .
\]

Hence  

\[
\boxed{\,x t+\frac{y}{t}=(x-1)(y-1)\,}\tag{1}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  Expressing \(S\) in the new variables  

\[
S=\frac{a}{b}+\frac{b}{c}+\frac{c}{d}+\frac{d}{a}
   =x+\frac{b}{c}+y+\frac{d}{a}
   =x+y+\frac{t}{y}+\frac{1}{x t}.          \tag{2}
\]

Introduce  

\[
u:=x t\;(>0),\qquad\text{so that}\qquad\frac{y}{t}= \frac{xy}{u}.
\]

Equation (1) becomes  

\[
u+\frac{xy}{u}=(x-1)(y-1).                \tag{3}
\]

Multiplying (3) by \(u\) gives a quadratic for \(u\):

\[
u^{2}-(x-1)(y-1)u+xy=0 .                  \tag{4}
\]

Its two roots satisfy \(u_{1}u_{2}=xy\).

From (2) and \(u=xt\) we get  

\[
S = x+y+\frac{u}{xy}+\frac{1}{u}.        \tag{5}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  The best choice of \(u\)

For fixed \(x,y\) the term \(\displaystyle\frac{u}{xy}+\frac{1}{u}\) is minimized by AM–GM:

\[
\frac{u}{xy}+\frac{1}{u}\ge 2\sqrt{\frac{1}{xy}}=
\frac{2}{\sqrt{xy}},
\]

with equality when \(u^{2}=xy\;(u=\sqrt{xy})\).

Thus the minimal possible value of \(S\) for given \(x,y\) is  

\[
S_{\min}(x,y)=x+y+\frac{2}{\sqrt{xy}}.   \tag{6}
\]

Equality in (6) forces \(u=\sqrt{xy}\). Substituting this into (4) gives  

\[
xy-(x-1)(y-1)\sqrt{xy}+xy=0
\Longrightarrow (x-1)(y-1)=2\sqrt{xy}.   \tag{7}
\]

Hence the global minimum of \(S\) will be attained when (7) holds.
