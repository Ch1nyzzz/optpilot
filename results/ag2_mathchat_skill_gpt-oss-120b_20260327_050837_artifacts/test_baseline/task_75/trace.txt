\[

\qquad n\in \mathbb Z_{\ge 1},
\]

consider a consecutive block  

\[
\{P(a),P(a+1),\dots ,P(a+b)\},\qquad b\ge 0 .
\]

The block is **fragrant** when every element shares a non‑trivial common divisor with the product of the remaining elements, i.e.

\[
\forall i\;(0\le i\le b):\;
\gcd\!\bigl(P(a+i),\;\prod_{\substack{0\le j\le b\\j\neq i}}P(a+j)\bigr)>1 .
\]

The task is to find the smallest possible size  

\[
|\,\{P(a),\dots ,P(a+b)\}\,| = b+1 .
\]
