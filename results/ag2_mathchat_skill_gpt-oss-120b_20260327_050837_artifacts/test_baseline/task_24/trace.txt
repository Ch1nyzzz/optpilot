1. **How the number of regions grows**

   When a new line segment is drawn inside the disk, it cuts the existing drawing at a certain number \(k\) of interior points (the points where it meets previously‑drawn segments).  
   Adding that segment splits exactly \(k+1\) existing regions, so the total number of regions increases by \(k+1\).

   Consequently, if we start with the two diameters (which already give 4 regions) and then add the 25 random chords, the final number of regions is  

   \[
   R = 4 + 25 + \underbrace{\text{(total number of interior intersections)}}_{\displaystyle X}.
   \]

   The term “\(25\)” appears because each of the 25 new chords contributes at least one new region, and the extra regions come from the intersections.

2. **Expected number of interior intersections**

   Let  

   * \(I_{d}\) = total intersections between the 25 random chords and the two fixed diameters,  
   * \(I_{c}\) = total intersections among the 25 random chords themselves.

   Then \(X = I_{d}+I_{c}\) and  

   \[
   \mathbb{E}[R]=4+25+\mathbb{E}[I_{d}]+\mathbb{E}[I_{c}]
                =29+\mathbb{E}[I_{d}]+\mathbb{E}[I_{c}].
   \]

   Because the chords are drawn independently, we can compute the expectations by linearity:

   \[
   \mathbb{E}[I_{d}] = 25\cdot 2\cdot p_{d},
   \qquad
   \mathbb{E}[I_{c}] = \binom{25}{2}\,p_{c},
   \]

   where  

   * \(p_{d}\) = probability that a random chord (with its endpoints in different quadrants) meets a given diameter,  
   * \(p_{c}\) = probability that two independent random chords (each with endpoints in different quadrants) intersect.
