Output: **Solution – Step‑by‑Step Reasoning**

1. **Identify the “minimum impact speed.”**  
   The smallest speed a comet can have when it reaches the Earth’s surface is the speed it would acquire if it fell from rest at an infinite distance (i.e., from “infinity”). In that case the comet is simply accelerated by Earth’s gravity, and its impact speed equals the **escape velocity** at the Earth’s surface.

2. **Escape‑velocity formula.**  
   For a body of mass \(M\) and radius \(R\), the escape speed at the surface is  

   \[
   v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}},
   \]

   where \(G\) is the gravitational constant.

3. **Kinetic energy at impact.**  
   The kinetic energy (the energy that will be released on impact) of a comet of mass \(m\) moving at speed \(v_{\text{esc}}\) is  

   \[
   E = \frac12 m v_{\text{esc}}^{2}
       = \frac12 m \left(\frac{2GM}{R}\right)
       = \frac{mGM}{R}.
   \]

4. **Match with the given options.**  
   The expression \(\frac12 m (2GM/R)\) is exactly **option (C)**.

5. **Check the other options (for completeness).**  
   * (A) \(m v\) – missing the factor \(1/2\) and the correct velocity expression.  
   * (B) \(0.5\, m / R^{3}\) – dimensionally incorrect (energy ∝ mass × velocity², not 1/length³).  
   * (D) \(0.6\, G M^{2}/R\) – this is the approximate gravitational binding energy of the Earth, not the impact energy of a small comet.

**Answer:** **(C) \(\displaystyle \frac12\,m\left(\frac{2GM}{R}\right)\)** (which simplifies to \(mGM/R\)).
