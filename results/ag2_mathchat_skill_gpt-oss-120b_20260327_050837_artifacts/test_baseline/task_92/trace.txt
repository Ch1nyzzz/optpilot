### 1.  Geometry set‑up  

Put the circumcircle of \(\triangle ABC\) as the unit circle in the complex plane and denote  

\[
A=a,\qquad B=b,\qquad C=c ,\qquad |a|=|b|=|c|=1 .
\]

The midpoint of \(AC\) is  

\[
M=\frac{a+c}{2}.
\]

The line \(AB\) is \(\{\,b+ t(a-b)\mid t\in\mathbb R\,\}\) and the line \(BC\) is
\(\{\,b+ s(c-b)\mid s\in\mathbb R\,\}\).

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2.  The points \(P\) and \(Q\)

Let  

\[
P=b+u(a-b),\qquad Q=b+v(c-b) \qquad (u,v\neq 0)
\]

be the second intersections of the circle \(\omega\) (the unique circle through
\(B\) and \(M\)) with the sides \(AB\) and \(BC\).

Because the four points \(B,M,P,Q\) are concyclic, the cross–ratio of the four
points on the line through \(B\) is real:

\[
\frac{(P-B)(M-B)}{(P-M)(B-B)}\;\Big/\;
\frac{(Q-B)(M-B)}{(Q-M)(B-B)}\in\mathbb R .
\]

Writing the points with the above parametrisation and simplifying gives a
single linear relation between \(u\) and \(v\):

\[
\boxed{\,v=\frac{1-u}{1-2u}\,}. \tag{1}
\]

--------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.  The point \(T\)

The quadrilateral \(BP T Q\) is a parallelogram, hence  

\[
\overrightarrow{BT}= \overrightarrow{PQ}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
T = B+ (Q-P)= b+v(c-b)-u(a-b).
\]

Using (1) and the fact that \(b\) lies on the unit circle we obtain

\[
T = \frac{(1-2u)a+(1-2u)c-2u\,b}{\,1-2u\,}. \tag{2}
\]
