### 1.  Reformulating the problem  

* We have \(n=2017\) pairwise disjoint circles.  
* For a pair of circles there are two **external common tangents**; each of them is a straight line segment whose endpoints are the two tangent points.  
* A tangent segment may be drawn only if it does **not intersect any other circle** and it does **not intersect any segment that has already been drawn**.  
* Luciano draws segments one after another until no further segment can be added.  
* The set that remains is therefore **maximal** with respect to the “non‑intersection’’ condition.

If we shrink every circle to a point (keep the same centre) and keep the same order of drawing, the condition “does not intersect any other circle’’ disappears, while the condition “does not intersect a previously drawn segment’’ stays exactly the same – we are now drawing straight segments between points, never allowing two of them to cross.

Conversely, if the circles are taken **very small** (their radii are negligible compared with the distances between their centres) then a tangent segment between two circles is *almost* the straight segment joining the two centres, and it will intersect another circle only when the corresponding straight segment would pass through the centre of that third circle.  
Thus, by choosing the radii sufficiently small we can make the combinatorial situation for circles **identical** to that for a set of points in the plane in *general position* (no three collinear, no three circles sharing a common tangent).

Hence the problem is equivalent to the classical one:

> For \(n\) points in the plane, no three collinear, what are the possible numbers of edges in a **maximal** non‑crossing straight‑edge drawing?
