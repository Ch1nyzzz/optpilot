Output: **Solution – step‑by‑step reasoning**

1. **Recall the definitions**  
   - A *homomorphic image* of a group \(G\) is any group \(H\) for which there exists a **surjective** homomorphism \(\varphi : G \to H\).  
   - A *factor group* (or *quotient group*) of \(G\) is a group of the form \(G/N\) where \(N\trianglelefteq G\) is a normal subgroup.

2. **First Isomorphism Theorem**  
   For any homomorphism \(\varphi : G \to H\) we have  
   \[
   G/\ker\varphi \;\cong\; \operatorname{im}\varphi .
   \]
   If \(\varphi\) is surjective, then \(\operatorname{im}\varphi = H\), so  
   \[
   H \cong G/\ker\varphi .
   \]

3. **Analyze Statement 1**  
   - Let \(H\) be a homomorphic image of \(G\). By definition there is a surjective homomorphism \(\varphi : G \to H\).  
   - Applying the First Isomorphism Theorem gives \(H \cong G/\ker\varphi\).  
   - Since \(\ker\varphi\) is a normal subgroup of \(G\), \(G/\ker\varphi\) is a factor (quotient) group of \(G\).  
   Hence **every homomorphic image of \(G\) is isomorphic to a factor group of \(G\)**.  
   → Statement 1 is **True**.

4. **Analyze Statement 2**  
   - From the argument above, each homomorphic image \(H\) is *isomorphic* to some factor group \(G/N\) (with \(N=\ker\varphi\)).  
   - Conversely, for any normal subgroup \(N\trianglelefteq G\) the natural projection \(\pi : G \to G/N\) is a surjective homomorphism, so \(G/N\) is itself a homomorphic image of \(G\).  
   - Therefore the collection of groups that appear as homomorphic images of \(G\) coincides, up to isomorphism, with the collection of factor groups of \(G\).  
   → Statement 2 is **True**.

5. **Conclusion**  
   Both statements are true, so the correct option is  

   **(A) True, True**.
