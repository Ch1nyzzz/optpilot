### 1.  What a good partition looks like  

Let  

\[
A=\{a_{1},a_{2},\dots ,a_{n}\}\qquad (a_{i}\in \mathbb Z_{>0})
\]

and let  

\[
\operatorname{lcm}(X)=\text{least common multiple of the elements of }X,
\qquad   
\gcd (Y)=\text{greatest common divisor of the elements of }Y .
\]

A partition \((A_{1},A_{2})\) of \(A\) is *good* iff  

\[
\operatorname{lcm}(A_{1})=\gcd (A_{2})=:d .
\tag{1}
\]

For a fixed integer \(d\) define  

\[
\begin{aligned}
D(d)&=\{a\in A\mid a\mid d\},\\
M(d)&=\{a\in A\mid d\mid a\}.
\end{aligned}
\]

If (1) holds, then every element of \(A_{1}\) must belong to \(D(d)\) and every
element of \(A_{2}\) must belong to \(M(d)\); consequently  

\[
A=D(d)\cup M(d),\qquad D(d)\cap M(d)=\{d\}\;( \text{if }d\in A).
\tag{2}
\]

Thus a good partition is completely determined by a value \(d\) that satisfies
(2) together with the choice of a non‑empty subset of \(D(d)\) whose l.c.m.
is exactly \(d\) and a non‑empty subset of \(M(d)\) whose g.c.d. is exactly
\(d\).

For a set \(A\) consisting only of powers of a single prime \(p\),

\[
A=\{p^{e_1},p^{e_2},\dots ,p^{e_n}\},
\]

the least common multiple of a subset is \(p^{\max e_i}\) and the greatest common divisor of a subset is
\(p^{\min e_i}\).  
Hence a partition \((A_1,A_2)\) is *good* iff there exists an exponent value \(t\) such that  

* every element with exponent \(<t\) lies in \(A_1\);
* every element with exponent \(>t\) lies in \(A_2\);
* the elements whose exponent equals \(t\) are split between the two parts, each part receiving at least one of them.

If the exponent \(t\) occurs \(m_t\) times in the whole set, the number of **unordered** good partitions
corresponding to this \(t\) is  

\[
\frac{2^{m_t}-2}{2}=2^{\,m_t-1}-1 .
\]

(We choose a non‑empty proper subset of the \(m_t\) copies; the complement gives the same unordered
partition, so we divide by two.)

Therefore, for a multiset of exponents \(\{m_t\}_{t}\) the total number of good partitions is  

\[
\boxed{\displaystyle
\sum_{t}\bigl(2^{\,m_t-1}-1\bigr)} .
\]

Our task is to obtain the value \(2015\) with the **fewest total elements**

\[
n=\sum_{t} m_t .
\]
